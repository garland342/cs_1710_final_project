What is a Food Desert?

USDA Definition: A census tract qualifies as a food desert if it meets low-income criteria and has limited access to supermarkets—defined as being more than 1 mile from a supermarket in urban areas, or more than 10 miles in most rural areas, with up to 20 miles in particularly remote or sparsely populated rural tracts.
